Phase 1 — Get pre-approved
A pre-approval letter gives you a clear budget and makes your offers competitive.
0/5
✓
Choose a loan type Important
Review our loan types page to understand Conventional, FHA, VA, and USDA options. Your AmeriDream loan officer can help you decide which fits your situation best.
✓
Submit your application Important
Apply online at AmeriDream — it takes about 10 minutes and there's no commitment. A loan officer will reach out quickly to walk you through next steps.
✓
Upload required documents
Your loan officer will request specific documents. The faster you provide them, the faster your pre-approval letter arrives — typically 24–48 hours.
✓
Review your Loan Estimate
Once pre-approved, review your Loan Estimate carefully. It details your interest rate, monthly payment, and estimated closing costs. Ask questions about anything unclear.
✓
Receive your pre-approval letter Milestone
Once you have this letter, you're officially ready to shop. Keep it handy — you'll need it when making offers. Most letters are valid for 60–90 days.
🏠
Phase 2 — Find your home
Now the fun part — house hunting with confidence and a clear budget.
0/6
✓
Choose a real estate agent
Find a buyer's agent who knows the North Texas market — The Colony, Decatur, and Bridgeport areas. A good agent costs you nothing as the seller pays their commission.
✓
Make your must-have list
Write down non-negotiables (bedrooms, school district, commute distance) vs. nice-to-haves. This keeps you focused and prevents emotional overspending.
✓
Tour homes in person
Photos can be deceiving. Always visit in person, check water pressure, look for signs of water damage, and assess the neighborhood at different times of day.
✓
Make a competitive offer Important
Your agent will help you price your offer strategically. Include your pre-approval letter — it signals to sellers that you're a serious, qualified buyer.
✓
Negotiate terms
Beyond price, negotiate closing date, repairs, appliances, and closing cost contributions. Your agent handles this on your behalf.
✓
Offer accepted — sign the contract Milestone
Congratulations! Once you're under contract, the clock starts on your closing timeline. Notify your AmeriDream loan officer immediately so they can begin final processing.
🔍
Phase 3 — Due diligence & underwriting
Inspections, appraisals, and final loan approval — the home stretch.
0/7
✓
Schedule a home inspection Important
Hire a licensed inspector to evaluate the home's condition. This typically costs $300–$500 and could save you thousands by uncovering hidden issues before you buy.
✓
Review inspection report
Go through the report with your agent. Decide which issues to negotiate repairs for, request credits, or accept as-is. Major structural or safety issues should be addressed.
✓
Home appraisal completed
Your lender orders an appraisal to confirm the home's value. If it comes in below the purchase price, you may need to renegotiate or cover the gap.
✓
Respond to lender requests promptly Important
Underwriting may request additional documents — respond quickly. Delays in this phase can push back your closing date.
✓
Purchase homeowner's insurance
Your lender requires proof of insurance before closing. Shop around for the best rate — your agent or loan officer may have recommendations for local providers.
✓
Review Closing Disclosure
You'll receive this at least 3 business days before closing. Compare it to your Loan Estimate — verify all numbers and ask your loan officer about any differences.
✓
Clear to close issued Milestone
When underwriting approves your loan, you'll receive a "clear to close." Your closing date is confirmed. Arrange your cashier's check or wire transfer for closing funds.
🎉
Phase 4 — Closing day & move in
The finish line — sign the papers and get your keys.
0/5
✓
Do a final walkthrough
Walk through the home 24 hours before closing to confirm it's in the agreed condition — repairs completed, seller's belongings removed, appliances working.
✓
Bring required items to closing Important
You'll need: government-issued photo ID, cashier's check or wire confirmation for closing costs and down payment, and any documents your lender requested.
✓
Sign closing documents
You'll sign a stack of documents — take your time reading each one. Don't hesitate to ask your loan officer or closing attorney to explain anything.
✓
Receive your keys 🗝️ Milestone
Once all documents are signed and funds are transferred, you'll receive your keys. You're officially a homeowner — congratulations!
✓
Change locks & update address
Change the locks on all entry doors immediately. Update your address with the USPS, your employer, bank, and any subscriptions. Set up utilities in your name.
