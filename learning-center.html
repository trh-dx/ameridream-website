Home purchase basics

The home buying process,
step by step.

1

Check your credit

Your credit score affects your interest rate and which loan programs you qualify for. Get a free copy of your credit report and review it for any errors before you apply.

2

Set your budget

Determine how much house you can comfortably afford — not just the purchase price, but also taxes, insurance, HOA fees, and maintenance. A good rule of thumb: keep housing costs under 28% of gross income.

3

Get pre-approved

A pre-approval letter from AmeriDream tells sellers you're serious and ready to buy. It shows your maximum loan amount and gives you a competitive edge when making offers.

4

Find your home

Work with a real estate agent to find homes that fit your budget and lifestyle. Once you find the right one, your pre-approval makes your offer stronger from the start.

5

Make an offer

Your agent will help you craft a competitive offer. Once accepted, you'll enter the contract period where inspections, appraisals, and final loan approval take place.

6

Close on your home

At closing, you'll sign the final paperwork, pay your closing costs and down payment, and receive the keys. AmeriDream will be with you every step of the way to make sure it goes smoothly.

Mortgage glossary

Common terms explained.

Pre-approval
A lender's conditional commitment to loan you a specific amount, based on a review of your income, assets, and credit. Stronger than pre-qualification.
Down payment
The upfront cash you pay toward the purchase price of a home. The remainder is financed through your mortgage.
APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
The total yearly cost of your loan including interest and fees, expressed as a percentage. Useful for comparing loans side by side.
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance)
Insurance that protects the lender if you default. Required on conventional loans with less than 20% down. Drops off once you hit 20% equity.
Escrow
An account held by your lender where a portion of your monthly payment is set aside to cover property taxes and homeowner's insurance.
Closing costs
Fees paid at the closing of a real estate transaction. Typically 2–5% of the loan amount and include appraisal, title, and origination fees.
Debt-to-income ratio (DTI)
Your total monthly debt payments divided by your gross monthly income. Lenders use this to assess your ability to manage monthly payments.
Fixed vs. adjustable rate
A fixed rate stays the same for the life of the loan. An adjustable rate (ARM) starts lower but can change after an initial period based on market rates.
